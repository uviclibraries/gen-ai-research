# GenAI Introduction & Prompt Design: A Double-Edged Sword

**Time plan:** Pre-work 25 min • Mini-lecture 15 min • Hands-on 65 min

GenAI can accelerate good work—or amplify weak work. This workshop gives you simple prompts, safety rules, and practice so you can use GenAI productively without undermining your judgment or academic integrity.

You'll start with **prompt design** and the **ethics/safety** basics that matter in student research. In the hands-on segment, you'll evaluate GenAI outputs for **accuracy** and **bias**, and you'll see how UVic policies shape responsible use.

---

## What you'll learn (learning objectives)

By the end of this **introductory** workshop, you will be able to:

1. **Explain GenAI** and list where it helps in academic work.
2. **Write better prompts** using the CRAFT framework — audience, format, constraints, and evidence requests (Activities 1 & 2).
3. **Generate and structure research ideas** using a diverge → cluster → converge pipeline (Activity 3).
4. **Run a claim audit** — ask for claims, verify with independent sources, and correct errors (Activity 4).
5. **Protect your data** by adjusting privacy settings, redacting PII, and reading T&Cs with a safety mindset (Activity 5).
6. **Use GenAI for practical tasks** (brainstorming, outlining, formatting) without breaching academic integrity.
7. **Give pre-submit feedback** on your own assignments using a structured rubric (Activity 6).

> **This workshop focuses on prompt design, critical evaluation, and data safety.** For specialized AI-powered literature search tools, see the **[Literature Searching with AI](https://lib.uvic.ca/ai-lit-search)** workshop. For AI's environmental impact, see the **[AI and the Environment](https://uviclibraries.github.io/genai-pathway/#introductory-workshops)** workshop.

## The Prompt Design Loop

1. **Idea / Goal** — what outcome do you actually need?
2. **Prompt** — instructions + context + constraints
3. **Result** — model output
4. **Test** — is it correct, usable, safe, complete?
5. **Feedback + Refine** — tighten constraints, add examples, fix format
